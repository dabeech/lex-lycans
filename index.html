Lexington Lycans and All American Karate Studio

Welcome, friend, to the martial way. Here you have a choice to make - do you take the path of open hand fighting, or the path of fully armored combat? Shotokan Karate or Buhurt? These two paths are both fulfilling in their own ways. If you choose the path of the open hand, Shotokan Karate, you will learn defense and discipline. If you choose the path of armored fighting, Buhurt, you will learn to fight with steel and board to take down your armored opponents. Either way, you will become stronger. Either way, you will find friends in this group to fight by your side. And if you can't choose one, you can always choose both.

So what is it? Karate or armored combat? Scroll below for information.

Shotokan Karate

By choosing this path, you will learn the ways of Shotokan - a traditional and influential form of karate developed by master Gichin Funakoshi.

Our Shotokan classes focus on self-defense, stances, breath control, and traditional open hand katas and techniques. We also teach our students how to use weapons such as bo, nunchaku, sai, and kama.

Students will be tested on their knowledge and application of these techniques, receiving Belt Rank Certifications upon passing.

Shotokan classes are held Mondays and Wednesdays. Kids classes (age 4-17) start at 6pm, and Adults classes (18+) start at 7pm.

Buhurt: Armored Combat

By choosing this path, you will learn the ways of Buhurt - full contact armored fighting. Suit up in full armor and take down your opponent using blunted weapons and various mixed-martial arts techniques.

Fights take two forms: 1 on 1 Duels that pit you against a single opponent, and Team Melees consisting of 3, 5, 10, or even 100 knights on each side.

Our team regularly attends Renaissance Fairs and Tournaments, each event bringing with it a chance to travel and make lasting friends along the way.

Buhurt classes are held on Thursdays from 6-8pm.
